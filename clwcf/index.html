Our Mission & Vision

We strive to reduce cancer mortality rate by increasing public awareness and initiating preventive measures.

  • Advocacy

    Fighting for better cancer care in Nigeria as well as globally, ensuring every child has access to quality treatment.

  • Access to Treatment and Support

    Every cancer patient deserves access to the best treatment, care, and support available.

  • Awareness and Education

    Encouraging early detection through screenings, diagnosis, treatment, and ongoing support.

About CLWCF

CHILDREN LIVING WITH CANCER FOUNDATION (CLWCF) is a Non Government Organization (NGO) set up to cater for the welfare, and medication of children battling with cancer whilst providing support for their families. We are registered with ICCCPO, an international organization dedicated to the care of children with cancer, and those that have survived cancer.

We seek creative and engaging methods to create awareness about the plight of children living with cancer as well as raise funds for the foundation for the benefit of affected kids.

CLWCF is committed to help facilitate awareness of childhood cancers and support of families affected by cancer.

Children Living With Cancer Foundation (CLWCF) is a Non-Government Organization (NGO) established to provide comprehensive support for children battling cancer and their families.

FAQs

01

What types of children with cancer do you support?

+

We support children with all types of cancer including leukemia, brain tumors, lymphoma, sarcomas, and other pediatric cancers. Our programs are designed to provide comprehensive care regardless of the specific diagnosis.

02

How can families access your services?

+

Families can contact us directly through our helpline, get referred by healthcare providers, or reach out through our website. We have intake coordinators who help assess needs and connect families with appropriate services.

03

Do you provide financial assistance?

+

Yes, we offer various forms of financial assistance including help with medical bills, transportation costs, accommodation expenses, and emergency financial support for families facing hardship due to their child's cancer treatment.

04

What support do you offer to siblings?

+

We recognize that cancer affects the entire family. We offer sibling support groups, counseling services, educational programs, and special activities designed to help brothers and sisters cope with their sibling's illness.

05

How can I volunteer or get involved?

+

There are many ways to get involved including volunteering at events, becoming a mentor, fundraising, spreading awareness, or joining our advocacy efforts. Contact our volunteer coordinator to learn about current opportunities.
